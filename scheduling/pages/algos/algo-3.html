Round Robin Algorithm

Round Robin Algorithm is designed for making a time-sharing system in CPU scheduling. It is similar to FCFS but we have to say that preemption is added to FCFS which gives us Round Robin algorithm. A small unit of time is added, or we can say a process will be allowed only that amount of time for the first round and then will be given to next process in wait. This time is called time quantum. Time quantum may have different values as per user or systems.

Here New process are added to tail of ready queue, which leads to 2 different possibilities:
1) CPU burst time is less than time quantum
2) CPU burst time is more than time quantum, and accordingly the system will give the CPU to the processes.

    Advantages:

  • Every process gets an equal share of the CPU.
  • RR is cyclic in nature, so there is no starvation.
  • This scheduling method does not depend upon burst time. Thatâ€™s why it is easily implementable on the system.

    • DisAdvantages:

  • Setting the quantum too short, increases the overhead and lowers the CPU efficiency, but setting it too long may cause poor response to short processes.
  • Average waiting time under the RR policy is often long.
  • Finding a correct time quantum is a quite difficult task in this system.
